Social Connectedness Index

The Social Connectedness Index (SCI) helps answer a simple question: if you pick one person in one place and another person somewhere else, how likely are they to be friends?

Skip to tools Next ↓

What the number means

How intense are the social ties between two places?

The SCI is a globally comparable measure of the relative probability that two people in different locations are Facebook friends.

The SCI is relative.

If one SCI value is twice another, the friendship likelihood is twice as high. The exact units matter less than ratios and patterns.

The SCI is symmetric.

The connection from A to B is the same as the connection from B to A, because Facebook friendships are mutual links.

The SCI is privacy protected.

The public data are aggregated and differentially private. The tools never expose person-level friendship records.

What people use it for

The SCI turns social ties into evidence.

The papers describe a dataset built for researchers and policy teams, but the intuition is useful for anyone trying to understand how places are socially connected.

Markets and trade

Social ties can help explain where business relationships form. Prior SCI research finds that more connected places tend to trade more with each other.

Health and mobility

During COVID-19, social network structure helped researchers study how behavior and disease risk moved across places.

Migration and displacement

Friendship networks can reveal where people may have family, friends, or support systems when they move or are displaced.

Communities that cross lines

Administrative borders are not always social borders. SCI clusters show which regions behave like connected communities.

Before opening a tool

Three tips make the maps easier to read.

  1. Choose a starting place. Then read the map as "where are people here most connected?"
  2. Look for exceptions. The surprising bright spots are often more interesting than the obvious nearby ones.
  3. Use the tool that matches the question. Explore live, make a publication map, or cluster regions into connected communities.
