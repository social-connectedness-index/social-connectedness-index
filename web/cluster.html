About Connected Communities

This tool groups sub-national regions into "clusters" using the strength of Facebook friendship ties between them. Two regions are treated as close when a randomly chosen Facebook user in one is highly likely to be friends with a randomly chosen user in the other.

It uses hierarchical agglomerative clustering (Ward's method): every region starts on its own and the two groups whose merger keeps clusters most compact are joined repeatedly, until the chosen number of clusters remain. The distance between two regions is the inverse of their Social Connectedness Index. No geographic constraint is applied — clusters are usually contiguous because connectedness falls off with distance, but a genuinely strong long-distance tie (e.g. migration links) can put non-adjacent regions in the same cluster.

More details, including the full data download, are at the Humanitarian Data Exchange.
